Overview

In 2019, Harry's was in active M&A discussions with global CPG companies.

Company Y reached out to our CEOs, Jeff and Andy, and asked us to attend their Global Leadership Conference and showcase what makes us special. Jeff and Andy agreed, and tapped me to run point.

We had six weeks to execute on an ambitious, high stakes project.

Objectives & Goals

Big picture: get acquired.

Big picture

Get acquired. Increase positive sentiment toward Harry's as a compelling brand to bring into the portfolio.

Short term

Leverage the audience, Company Y's CEO and 300 most senior leaders, to increase positive sentiment toward Harry's as a compelling brand to acquire and gain meaningful traction in M&A conversations.

Define, Plan, Execute

What makes us special?

01
Define: Understand the problem

Showcasing Harry's journey in retail was a no brainer. We shaped the new era of Target personal care aisles as a place for brand discovery. I quickly vetted this option and brought the finding back to Jeff and Andy: this route was expensive, with definite shipping risk. We aligned to pivot.

We pivoted to CX and digital acquisition. Our customer service had been best-in-class since day one. Katie Ingram was the first person to welcome you to the Harry's family when you placed your first order. The catch: Katie isn't real. She's our entire CX team, a brand persona embodying our brand ethos and a lever in our ability to forge brand loyalty online. This is what we wanted to share.

Our second workshop was a wine tasting for soap, to showcase our unique ability to drive bath and body sales online through an engaging digital experience.

02
Plan: Map the work and milestones

Develop strategy and approach with CEOs. Establish the team who would own delivery of content. Initiate our operating model. Identify milestones. Prioritize time with stakeholders early to invest in relationships, buy-in, and get this group energized about the overall goal.

03
Execute: Comms, tools, staying organized

Team syncs were biweekly, in-person, kept sacred. Weekly status to stakeholders: what did we get done, what deliverables are up next, blockers. Documentation via one Google Sheet that housed run-of-show, action items and owners, scripts, POs, budget. Vendor management ran on daily standups and VC walkthroughs.

Were we successful?

Delivery of workshop on time and on budget. Highest rated session among attendees.

Extremely positive reception from Company Y's CEO. Although it didn't end exactly as planned, Harry's eventually agreed to merge with a different CPG company, but has since taken its own path.

Harry's workshop in session · Global Leadership Conference

We created a women's shaving brand, expanded into personal care, acquired Lume deodorant, and even launched a cat brand. As of March 2024, Harry's officially filed paperwork to IPO. Although it didn't end exactly as planned, I like to think this event, and this period in Harry's history, was a critical turning point in understanding who we are as a business.

Challenges & learnings.

Culture clash

Adapting to process-driven, often less flexible corporate working rhythms like Company Y's is challenging for startups who pride themselves on absorbing learnings quickly to adapt continuously.

Lack of comms

Gaining buy-in across the team while balancing discretion and sensitive issues around an M&A was challenging in a high-transparency culture.

Iterate, learn, repeat

With such a high speed and high stakes project, it was important to fully vet things like our retail narrative quickly to prevent pivots or major blockers further downstream.
