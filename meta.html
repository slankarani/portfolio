Critical Alignment & the Notifications Design Vision Sprint

RoleDesign Operations Lead
TimelineThree weeks
Mandate fromHead of Facebook
Overview

A fragmented ecosystem needed a unified direction.

The Facebook Notifications ecosystem had become highly fragmented, resulting in a noisy, unclear user experience without a unified direction. Operations were led for a comprehensive Notifications Design Vision Sprint to define a clear call to arms and establish a cohesive long-term product direction.

Following the exit presentation of these visual outputs, the Head of Facebook, Tom Alison, directly requested an accelerated, high-visibility prototype to immediately de-risk and pressure-test the vision.

Objective

A direct mandate from the Head of Facebook.

Strategic

Rapidly translate a massive, long-term design vision into a functional prototype to fulfill a direct mandate from the Head of Facebook, stabilizing a historically fragmented core product area.

Functional

Build an immediate, high-velocity operational framework to keep cross-functional partners aligned under intense leadership scrutiny while protecting product quality.

Approach

A multi-layered operational model designed to maintain velocity without compromising execution standards.

01
Proactive Risk Elevation

Anticipated and elevated flags and risks early, providing crucial visibility into potential downfalls and enabling cross-functional leads to resolve alignment blocks in real time.

02
Cross-Functional Resourcing

Leveraged the Design Director's endorsement to strategically recruit specialized designers and engineers from the Design Systems team, ensuring the rapid-turnaround build kept quality, craft, and accessibility centered.

03
Structured Milestone Management

Partnered closely with Product and Engineering leads to set rigorous expectations and track daily progress, keeping point-of-contacts strictly on schedule.

Were we successful?

Delivered in three weeks. The model became the playbook.

The cross-functional squad was unblocked to execute and deliver the finalized prototype to executive leadership in exactly three weeks, setting a new standard for how the organization approaches high-stakes leadership asks.

The rapid collaboration model was codified into an official organizational playbook, later adopted and replicated by adjacent priority teams, including Immersive Facebook, to kick off their own demo v-teams.

"Saara's leadership helped invent a new collaboration model that is now
a playbook for other teams."

Design Director, Meta

Challenges & learnings.

Ambiguity is the operating condition, not the exception

Faced with extreme ambiguity and a high-pressure timeline, the work was structuring clarity fast enough to keep the team moving, making the operating model visible, explicit, and simple enough to hold under pressure.

Risk elevation is a leadership act

Surfacing flags early, before they became blockers, was what kept the timeline intact. Waiting for problems to surface on their own would have cost the team days it did not have.

A well-run sprint can outlast the sprint itself

The process became the product. The collaboration model built for this moment was adopted by other teams precisely because it was documented and transferable, not just effective in the moment.
